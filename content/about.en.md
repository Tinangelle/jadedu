Hello. I am currently studying psychology and am the founder and indie developer at Ignis Solaris Studio.

My résumé may look eclectic: game testing, a fair amount of writing, and recently—thanks to one course—a full-blown obsession with ethology. But to me it is all one thing: I like to observe how things actually work, then reconstruct them in my own way.

## Observing and Reconstructing Nature

An elective led me to build rigorous ethograms for grizzly and polar bears, and I was hooked. I tend to record animal behavior in a quantitative, non-anthropomorphic way. As my understanding deepens, I see humans less as masters of the ecosystem and more as participants in it. My interest in nature is not sentimental overflow—it is curiosity about a logic of coexistence grounded in respect.

## Indie Games & UX

I am developing a 2D simulation in Godot called *Let the Whale Breach*. The game carries my thinking about nature and animal behavior: how industry and the natural world might find a balanced middle ground while honoring innate animal needs. My psychology background also gives me an extra lens for UX—turning underlying logic into interaction that feels natural.

## FQA & Bug Hunting

I spent two full years as a functional quality assurance (FQA) tester. That high-intensity work made me hypersensitive to logic gaps and very comfortable hunting anomalies under strict specs. Now that I write code myself, that instinct for validating system stability comes in handy every day.

## Writing and the “Survival” of Idealism

Beyond code and research, I keep writing. I like to tease apart inner logic and motivation in prose. I have come to think that true idealists are like resilient “cockroaches”: strip away romantic filters, and they still show fierce vitality and stress tolerance in the void. Writing is how I sort out that tension, clear my head, and debrief my own thinking.
